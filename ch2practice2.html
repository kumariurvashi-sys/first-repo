Procrastination

Procrastination is a complex emotional regulation issue rather than just poor time management or laziness, affecting around 20% of adults by causing them to delay tasks despite knowing it will cause stress. Driven by the brain's desire to avoid discomfort (fear of failure, anxiety, boredom), it creates a cycle of temporary relief, guilt, and reduced long-term well-being. Overcoming it involves reducing task friction, such as breaking tasks into small, manageable steps.

Why we procrastinate ?

• Emotional Regulation : Procrastination is the brain's attempt to provide short-term comfort by avoiding tasks that feel overwhelming, anxiety-inducing, or boring.

• Self protection mechanism : Procrastination is the brain's attempt to provide short-term comfort by avoiding tasks that feel overwhelming, anxiety-inducing, or boring.

• Perfectionism : The feeling that a task must be done perfectly can create a mental block, leading to total avoidance.

• Biological Factors : The limbic system (emotional center) often overrides the prefrontal cortex (rational center), prioritizing immediate pleasure over future benefit.

The impact of procrastination

• Mental Health : Make daunting tasks smaller and less intimidating, suggest Verywell Mind.

• The 10-minute Rule : Commit to working on a task for just 10 minutes; often, starting is the hardest part, and motivation follows action.

• Forgive Yourself : Beating yourself up over past procrastination increases anxiety and makes you more likely to procrastinate again.
